Assessment Pattern



  • End sem exam will have part A & B comprising entire syllabus.
  • Part A will have 10 questions, out of which 8 to be answered [8 x 5 = 40 marks].
  • Part B will have 8 questions, out of which 6 to be answered [6 x 10 = 60 marks].
  • 100 marks will be weighted for 50%.
  • Duration of end sem exam will be 180 minutes.

Attendance

“Attendance policy is applicable to only those students who are registered for the particular semester and working in projects through the duration of the semester. 
Students who are on leave during the semester like ML/PML/Medical/LOP or ANY OTHER are ineligible to attend the semester as WISTA is a Work Integrated Learning Program
Students should refer the WILP Leave Policy for further details.”


    1. A student will be called upon to undergo studies during the hours and days as may be fixed by the Academy from time to time. Normally, the study hours would be from 8:30am to 6:00pm from Monday to Saturday / Sunday. 
      Classes are usually conducted on Saturdays/Sundays (depending on the location). In case the Class is conducted on weekday or if Saturday/Sunday is declared to be a working day, attending WiSTA classes Supersedes Business Unit requirement. Monday to Friday is usually reserved for "on the job" training. Further on such days students need to Swipe in/swipe out for recording attendance.

    1. To appear for the exams, a student needs to maintain an average attendance of 62.5% (40 sessions out of the 64 ) cutting across all the 4 subjects with a minimum attendance of 9 sessions in each subject. Not attending classes for reasons like medical /project work / late coming/personal problems and other reasons would be treated as absenteeism.

    1. The attendance status will be circulated via email periodically.

      b. Will have to register again for the same semester, with the subsequent batch.

Dissertation


    1. Identification of Topic: The dissertation topic must preferably be related to the ongoing project and the M.Tech Program. The quantum of work should justify 16 weeks worth of effort to be put in by the student. The topic should be acceptable to VIT and also add value to the organization.

    1. Identification of Supervisor: Depending on the topic/subject of dissertation a supervisor has to be chosen from within the work environment.

    1. Dissertation Outline : After identifying the topic and supervisor student has to submit a "proposal" stating the aim/objective of the dissertation to VIT in the prescribed format. The duly completed "Dissertation Outline” should reach the VIT Coordinator within the stipulated date.

    1. Dissertation work has to be carried out by each individual separately. Team work is not permitted.

    1. If a student’s CGPA is less than 5.5, the student will not be permitted to register for the dissertation. He/she has to re-appear for exams and secure a CGPA of 5.5 before taking up the dissertation. Also, a student with as F grade in any course will not be permitted to register for the Dissertation.

  1. Registration has to be completed by submitting a signed Registration Card at your respective location, where you have completed your 7th Semester.
The dissertation has to be completed strictly as per the guidelines outlined by VIT.

Extended Period of M.Tech Program

Any student opting to take an extended break (leave beyond permissible limits as stipulated in the academic study regulations of the M.Tech Program) due to medical reasons / marriage / maternity will be governed by the rules and regulations prevailing at the time of rejoining.

Fees


    1. Fees for all the semesters of the M.Tech program (including prospectus and registration fees) will be borne by Wipro. Please note that Wipro will bear fees for the 8 semesters. If a student re-registering / reappearing beyond 8 semesters the fees for the same should be borne by the student.

    1. Students discontinuing their studies (either voluntarily or being asked to discontinue) are subjected to the conditions laid down in the Enrollment Letter, the WISTA Bond agreement and the "WISTA Ready Reckoner".

    1. When a student has to re appear for an exam because he /she did not have a minimum CGPA of 5.5, the examination fees should be borne by the student.

    1. When a student has to reappear for the exam because he/she had an `F’ grade, the examination fees has to be borne by the student.

  1. When a student has to reappear for the exam because of attendance shortage, fees has to be borne by the student.

Overseas Travel

  • WiSTAites are not eligible for onsite travel for short / long term.
  • WiSTAites can go onsite after successful completion of M.Tech program.
  • WiSTAites enjoy the status of student therefore are not employees.
  • As per Immigration requirement, evidence needs to be established for employer-employee relationship before filling H-1 work permit, which WiSTAites cannot satisfy.
  • H-1B work permit for Short/long term can be filed only for Specialist skills which are not available in the US market.

Examination


~Mid Term / End semester /Guide lines ~/ Minimum academic requirements / Make up exam

Examinations are conducted by VIT as per their guidelines. There are two examinations for each semester: mid-semester exam and end-semester exam.

As per the VIT policy, there is no fixed pattern for question papers. The instructor in charge of a course decides the same in consultation with the faculty teaching the course.

The grading for all the courses across locations will be done centrally at VIT.


Mid semester Exam:

This exam covers the 50% of the syllabus.

It is a closed book exam

Any study kind of study materials, text books, prescribed books,loose sheets of material are not permitted to be carried to the exam hall.


End semester Exam:

This is a comprehensive exam and covers the entire syllabus.

It is a closed book exam.

Any study kind of study materials, text books, prescribed books,loose sheets of material are not permitted to be carried to the exam hall.


Guidelines :

No books/material and other information is permitted inside the exam hall during the examination.

Use of laptops, PCs, palmtops, mobile phones andany other electronic device irrespective of their size and capacity to store and retrieve digital information is not permitted.

Students need to be present at the venue at least five minutes before the examination.

Kindly adhere to the seating arrangements made in the examination hall

Writing with pencil is not allowed. All the rough work should be done only on the answer sheet.

The grades awarded by VIT faculty are final.
