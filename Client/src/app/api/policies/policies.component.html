Employee State Insurance (ESI)

    1. Introduction: This is in line with the Employee State Insurance Act, 1948. This is a social welfare legislation enacted primarily with the object of providing certain benefits in contingencies such as sickness, maternity, temporary or permanent physical disablement and death due to employment injury resulting in loss of wages or earning capacity. The Act guarantees medical care to you and your immediate dependants.

  1. Eligibility:
    • If your monthly scholarship is below Rs.15,000/- per month, you are covered under the ESI Act. You would be required to submit the filled in application for ESIC benefits within a week of joining WiSTA program.
    • This benefit is over and above the other company benefits you are eligible for.
  2. Policy:
    • On receipt of the ESI card, you may take the eligible beneficiaries for treatment at an ESI centre / hospital unless referred to other hospital for claim under ESI.
    • The beneficiaries to the scheme are self, spouse, children, dependent parents, dependent family members such as brothers / sisters (below the age of 18 years) who will be entitled to claim medical benefits as stipulated by the ESI Act.
    The ESI benefits are completely managed by the Employees State Insurance Corporation (“ESIC”) directly. You are requested to approach the ESIC for any further information on benefits or details for claiming the benefits.

        1. You are assigned to work in Early Morning and Late Evening shifts. Night shift working is normally not applicable to you .         1. You work for a minimum of 4 hours between 10 pm and 7:30 am and
        2. You work for a minimum total duration of 9.5 continuous hours in a shift including a break period of 45 minutes
        3. Night shift working has been approved by SDH and WISTA Program Head.

Students in their first and second year of the M.Tech Program can opt for rotation to other projects within their business unit with prior approval from their project supervisor. In such cases students need not necessarily apply through Wings Within interface in myWipro.

Students in their third and f ourth year of the M.Tech Program can opt for rotation outside their business unit *through Wings Within, with the standard Wings Within eligibility criteria applicable. Such students need to apply through myWipro Wings Within norms.

M.Tech Nomeclature Change


Frequently Asked questions on MS to M.Tech Nomenclature change

In compliance with a direction from the University Grants Commission (UGC) to all universities vide its circular dated July 10, 2014, to align the nomenclature of the degrees awarded by them with the degrees specified by the UGC in The Gazette of India No. 27 dated July 5, 2014. The university will be renaming the degree earlier titled Master of Science (M.S.) offered through its Work Integrated Learning Programmes.


Specifically, the Master of Science (M.S.) Information Technology programme will be renamed as Master of Technology (M. Tech.) Information Technology. The M. Tech. Information Technology programme is academically equivalent to the corresponding M.S. Information Technology programme, in terms of content and rigour.


Below are the FAQs which will give you an understanding of the nomenclature change in detail,


    1. What is the sudden reason for change in the Degree Nomenclature?

UGC which is the highest policy making body in India for academics reviews higher education program from time to time. All Universities including IITs, NITs, and private universities have to comply with the educational guidelines provided by UGC.


    1. From which batch of WiSTA, does this nomenclature be effective?
      • All the WiSTA students who are entering their Master program with VIT starting April 2015 will be in M.Tech degree nomenclature.
      • All existing WiSTA students will continue to with MS (IT) Degree nomenclature.

    1. As M.Tech is not provided to degree holders (B.Sc. or BCA), what is the eligibility criteria to have the M.Tech Certificate with a B.Sc.?
      2. Yes, it is valid as the program designed in WILP is equivalent to post graduate degree in Engg, based on the program duration, curriculum, and academic rigor. The possible eligibility criteria for M Tech is listed below

Eligibility Criteria 1: 
One must be BE/B.Tech/MCA/M.Sc.(IT) with min of 16 years of schooling / studies (10+2+4) or (10+2+3+3)


Eligibility Criteria 2: 
One must be a graduate in Science course with 10+2+3 years of studies, must do 10+2+3 and (2+2) years of studies, which is substantiated through WiSTA 4-year studies.


Eligibility Criteria 3: 
One must be a post graduate in Science course with 10+2+3+2 years of studies, must do 10+2+3 + 2 + 2 (this is possible in IITs)


    1. Also, if MS is recognized as Master of Surgery in India, what is the value of the certificate which will be provided to the students who have already registered?

Universities have to align with the degree nomenclature as specified by UGC and therefore the degree that you are receiving would be valid.


    1. What will be scope of M.Tech degree for B.Sc/BCA degree holders? How will be this qualification of B.Sc. / BCA with M.Tech accepted outside WIPRO?

M.Tech is widely accepted across the academia, Govt and industry as it is UGC directive.


    1. Could a student enrolled in an M.S. programme seek a transfer to the corresponding M.Tech programme?

All exiting students of WiSTA will continue with MS (IT) and the batch commenced in April 2015 will get into M.Tech. There is no option to switch from MS to M.Tech nomenclature for the existing WiSTA batches.


    1. Will there be any effect on the H1 VISA application with GIMS team on the revised nomenclature?

Since all exiting WiSTA batches are going to get MS (IT), there will not be any concern on your H1B application. So as the new WISTA batch which is admitted to Master in April 2015, will get M.Tech degree. GIMS team is informed about the Program nomenclature change.


    1. If we are going for Higher Education (India/ Abroad), then which degree holds valid MS or M M.Tech?

Both are valid. Only nomenclature change is recommended by UGC.


    1. If any company/ Institute does Not accept M.Tech (Information Technology) as valid degree, then whether Wipro/ VIT University are taking any responsibility for the same? And will we be getting any formal document from University?

VIT is a University established under Section (3) of UGC, will provide the degree equivalence certificate in such situations.

Work integrated learning programs (WILP) are not only meet the academic requirements such as duration, min number of courses, number of semesters, and but also include industry oriented elective courses in the curriculum (towards internship).

M.Tech degree not as Integrated M.Tech.

All WiSTA batches (IT, Data Science, VLSI, and Engineering Design) which are getting into their Master’s program starting April 2015 will be in M.Tech nomenclature.

The Masters in Engineering Design Systems is a 5-year integrated program. So, your will receive M.Tech (Engineering Design Systems) degree. There is no BS nor B.Tech before completing M.Tech.
